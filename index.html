📧 The Spam & Ham Classifier

Supervised
💡 What's the ML idea here?

Supervised learning means learning from examples that come with the correct answers (labels). First you'll see training data — emails already stamped SPAM or HAM (not-spam). Your brain will pick up the patterns (ALL CAPS, "FREE!!!", sketchy senders…). Then you'll classify new, unlabeled emails — exactly what a trained spam filter does millions of times a day.

🌌 The Cluster Finder

Unsupervised
💡 What's the ML idea here?

Unsupervised learning gets no labels at all — just raw data. The algorithm's job is to discover hidden structure by itself. Here you'll run K-Means: drop K "centroids" (cluster centers), then each step the algorithm ① assigns every point to its nearest centroid and ② moves each centroid to the middle of its points. Repeat until nothing moves — the machine just found groups nobody told it about. (Real uses: customer segments, galaxy types, playlist vibes.)

🤖 The Bot Maze Trainer

Reinforcement
💡 What's the ML idea here?

Reinforcement learning = no labels, no answer key — just rewards and penalties. The bot wanders by trial and error; every move updates its Q-table ("how good is action A in cell S?"). Watch the amber glow spread backwards from the battery as knowledge propagates. Then try the evil experiment: set the fire penalty to −1 and retrain — the bot will happily take the fiery shortcut, because the bot learns whatever your rewards actually say, not what you meant. (This is how game AIs, robots, and chatbots get fine-tuned.)
