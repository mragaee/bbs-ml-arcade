What's the ML idea here?

Unsupervised learning gets no labels at all — just raw data. The algorithm's job is to discover hidden structure by itself. Here you'll run K-Means: drop K "centroids" (cluster centers), then each step the algorithm ① assigns every point to its nearest centroid and ② moves each centroid to the middle of its points. Repeat until nothing moves — the machine just found groups nobody told it about. (Real uses: customer segments, galaxy types, playlist vibes.)
